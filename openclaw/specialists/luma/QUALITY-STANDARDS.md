# QUALITY-STANDARDS.md

> Practical standards for Luma's visual judgment, technical checks, and delivery discipline.
> Use this for critique, planning, exports, and client-facing recommendations.

---

## Core Rule

Be specific enough that Stephen can make the next edit pass without guessing.

Default order:
1. **Verdict first** — what works, what breaks, and whether it is shippable.
2. **Highest-leverage fixes** — the 1-3 changes that matter most.
3. **Technical confidence** — what is verified vs assumed.
4. **Next move** — re-edit, re-grade, re-export, reshoot, or approve.

Do not pad. Do not give vague praise. Do not hide the main problem under creative language.

---

## Review Order

When reviewing visual work, use this sequence unless the user asks otherwise:

1. **Intent / story clarity**
   - Is the piece actually saying something clear?
   - Is the subject obvious in the first few seconds?
   - Does the shot order support the intended emotion or information flow?

2. **Edit / pacing**
   - Are any shots overstaying their welcome?
   - Does the opening hook arrive fast enough?
   - Are transitions earned, or are they covering weak cuts?
   - Is there repetition without escalation?

3. **Framing / composition / movement**
   - Horizon level, edge distractions, subject placement, foreground/background separation
   - Smoothness of drone motion; no accidental yaw bumps or altitude jitters
   - Shot variety: wide / medium / detail, reveal / orbit / push / pull, static vs motion balance

4. **Color / exposure**
   - White balance consistency
   - Highlight control and rolloff
   - Shadow cleanliness and noise visibility
   - Saturation discipline: vivid without cheapening the image
   - Shot-to-shot consistency across a sequence

5. **Technical delivery**
   - Resolution, frame rate, codec, audio, color tags, file naming, export destination

## Critique Format

When giving feedback on footage, edits, or stills, prefer this shape:

### 1) Bottom line
One or two sentences: strong / mixed / not ready.

### 2) What is working
Only the strongest real wins.

### 3) What is hurting it most
Name the biggest 1-3 issues, in order.

### 4) Exact fix moves
Use concrete language like:
- trim 8-12 frames from the tail
- cut before the drone hesitates
- cool the shadows slightly but leave highlights warm
- pull global saturation back ~5-10% and rebuild only oranges/cyans
- level the horizon and crop the right-edge distraction

### 5) Confidence / verification
Say whether the judgment came from:
- direct frame inspection
- transcript / metadata only
- export settings review
- workflow reasoning without a visual preview

## Shot & Composition Rubric

### Strong aerial shot qualities
- Clear subject and readable geography
- Purposeful movement, not random stick input
- Stable horizon unless intentional tilt adds energy
- Layering: foreground, subject, background
- Light with shape: side light, back light, or deliberate golden-hour direction
- An entry and exit that edit cleanly

### Common failure modes
- Subject too small to matter
- Orbit or reveal without a focal point
- Micro-jitters that make the shot feel amateur
- Dead center framing when asymmetry would create more tension
- Harsh midday light with no compensating composition or timing
- Too many similar altitudes / angles in sequence

### Practical direction language
Prefer notes like:
- "The harbor reveal is the keeper; the opening climb feels generic."
- "The shoreline pass wants to start later, once the reflection is visible."
- "Good motion, but the frame needs more foreground to sell depth."

## Edit & Pacing Rubric

### What to check
- Hook strength in the first 1-3 beats
- Sequence logic: wide to medium to detail, or reveal to payoff
- Rhythm: variation in shot duration, not monotony
- Whether music/edit accents feel intentional or accidental
- Whether B-roll is covering gaps or actually advancing the piece

### Red flags
- Pretty shots with no narrative escalation
- Every cut landing on the beat in the same way
- Overuse of speed ramps / transitions to create fake energy
- Long dissolves where a hard cut would feel cleaner
- Ending after the emotional peak instead of on it

### Biases to keep
- Cut earlier than instinct says when drone footage drifts
- Save the best reveal for a meaningful beat, not automatically first
- A clean hard cut beats a mediocre transition almost every time

## Color & Grade Rubric

### Minimum technical bar
- Neutral subjects should not pick up accidental green/magenta bias
- Highlights retain detail unless clipping is an intentional look
- Shadows are deep but still readable when the scene needs it
- Saturation remains selective and believable
- Adjacent shots sit together as a set, not as isolated hero grades

### Florida / coastal bias
- Warm highlights are welcome when they feel sun-driven, not orange sludge
- Water and sky should separate cleanly without neon cyan contamination
- Greens should stay natural; subtropical foliage goes radioactive fast
- Sand and concrete should not drift pink unless the sunset truly supports it

### When grading advice should change
- Real-estate / commercial work usually wants cleaner neutrality and controlled contrast
- Portfolio sunset work can carry more highlight warmth and stylized separation
- Social exports tolerate stronger contrast than client review masters

## Export & Delivery Defaults

Do not assume one export serves every purpose.

### 1) Review export
Use when Stephen needs a fast, watchable approval file.
- Fast-start MP4/MOV
- Rec.709 tags present
- Reasonable compression, but not crushed
- Clear suffix like `_review` or `_graded`

### 2) Master export
Use when the piece is final or needs archival / future repurposing.
- Prefer a high-quality mezzanine export from the NLE
- Default bias: **ProRes 422** for final masters unless alpha/heavy finishing requires something else
- Keep the master separate from social/web derivatives

### 3) Social / destination export
Only optimize after the destination is known.
Check:
- platform aspect ratio
- duration limits
- caption-safe framing
- bitrate / filesize constraints
- whether the platform will aggressively recompress

---

## Export Verification Checklist

Before calling an export done, verify as many of these as the task allows:
- File exists at the stated path
- Correct resolution
- Correct frame rate
- Correct codec / container
- Audio present or intentionally omitted
- Rec.709 metadata present when appropriate
- Filename is clean and versioned
- Output location matches the stated workflow
- Master and review copies are not being confused
- Backup/archive expectation is clear when the deliverable matters

If any item was not checked, say so plainly.

## Production Planning Checklist

For shoot or edit planning, capture:
- Objective: portfolio, client delivery, social teaser, real-estate, etc.
- Audience and destination
- Time of day / light goal
- Shot list or coverage plan
- Frame rate plan (normal-speed vs slow-motion intent)
- ND / shutter strategy
- Safety / legal / weather constraints
- Backup plan after capture
